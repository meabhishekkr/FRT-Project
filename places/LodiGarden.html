Lodi Gardens
overview

Lodi Gardens is a city park situated in New Delhi, India.Spread over 90 acres.

It contains Mohammed Shah's Tomb, the Tomb of Sikandar Lodi, the Shisha Gumbad and the Bara Gumbad,[2] architectural works of the 15th century by Lodis - who ruled parts of northern India.

In the middle of the gardens is the Bara Gumbad ("Big Dome"), it consists of a large rubble-construct dome and is not a tomb[3] but was constructed as a gateway to either the attached three domed masjid (mosque) or a large walled enclosure.
